The Korean won weakened to 1,480.1 per U.S. dollar at the 15:30 Seoul benchmark fixing in its most recent session, a rise of 6.7 won in the pair that leaves the currency parked near the weakest levels of the current stretch. The move extends a run in which the won has repeatedly failed to pull back below the 1,470s, underscoring persistent downward pressure on the currency.

## Where the Fixing Landed

Across the latest run of trading days, the 15:30 reference rate — the benchmark Seoul uses to mark the won against the dollar each afternoon — printed a tight but deteriorating sequence. One session settled at 1,473.4, a 5.0-won firming for the won on the day. A subsequent fixing came in at 1,478.4, essentially flat at 0.1 won weaker. The most recent print jumped 6.7 won to 1,480.1, the softest of the three closes.

That final move is the one that matters for direction: a 6.7-won daily depreciation is a meaningful single-session slide, and it pushed the pair to the top of its recent range rather than back toward the stronger 1,473 handle seen only a couple of sessions earlier.

## A Currency Stuck in a Weak Band

The three closing marks — 1,473.4, 1,478.4 and 1,480.1 — trace a narrow corridor of roughly seven won. Within that band, however, the bias has been toward a weaker won: an early bounce that took the rate down to 1,473.4 was steadily given back, first drifting to 1,478.4 and then breaking higher to 1,480.1.

For an economy that exports heavily, a won this close to 1,480 per dollar is a double-edged number. It flatters won-denominated export revenues on paper, but it also raises the local-currency cost of imported energy and inputs and signals that capital is not rewarding won assets at these levels. The inability to hold gains below 1,475 is the more telling detail than any single day's move.

## What the Fixings Point To

With the benchmark now sitting at 1,480.1, the immediate reference points are clear: 1,473.4 marks the strong end of the recent range and 1,480 the weak end. A close that pushes decisively above 1,480 would confirm the won is probing new lows for the period, while a return toward the mid-1,470s would suggest the latest 6.7-won drop was a spike rather than a fresh trend. For now, the afternoon fixings describe a currency under steady, if orderly, pressure.
