South Korea has settled on the site for its planned Honam semiconductor cluster, choosing the grounds of the Gwangju military airport to anchor what could become one of the country's largest regional industrial projects. Multiple Korean press signals reported the site decision on the same afternoon, with the presidential office cited as the source of the determination.

## What Was Decided

The core outcome is a location decision: the cluster will be developed on the Gwangju military airport site rather than an alternative venue. Yonhap's headline feed and Hankyung both carried the same essential point — that the Honam semiconductor cluster is set to be established on the military airport grounds — while Hankyung specifically attributed the decision to the presidential office (청와대). The consistency across independent outlets indicates a formal government announcement rather than an unconfirmed proposal.

Choosing the military airport site is significant because it repurposes a large, contiguous parcel of land already under public control, a factor that typically shortens the timeline for site acquisition and zoning compared with assembling private holdings.

## The Reported Scale

ChosunBiz framed the undertaking as an "800 trillion won semiconductor cluster." That figure — roughly on the order of several hundred billion U.S. dollars at prevailing exchange rates — comes from press reporting rather than an itemized official disclosure, so it should be read as the reported headline scale of the initiative rather than a verified line-item budget. No breakdown of public versus private capital, phasing, or timeline was contained in the available signals.

At that reported magnitude, the project would sit alongside the country's largest existing chip-cluster commitments and would mark a deliberate push to extend advanced-manufacturing investment into the Honam region, which has historically trailed the Seoul metropolitan area and the central corridor in semiconductor capacity.

## Why the Location Matters

Placing a semiconductor cluster in Honam addresses a long-standing regional-balance objective in Korean industrial policy: distributing high-value manufacturing beyond the established chip belt. Gwangju, as the region's largest city, offers an existing urban labor pool and infrastructure base, and converting the military airport site ties the industrial plan to the separately debated question of relocating that airbase.

## What Remains Unconfirmed

The available signals establish the site and the reported headline scale but leave the operational details open. Not specified in these reports are the anchor tenants or lead chipmakers, the construction and production timeline, the split between government and corporate funding, and how the airbase relocation required to free the land will proceed. These elements will determine whether the 800 trillion won figure reflects committed investment or an aspirational ceiling, and readers should treat the number as a reported projection until official disclosures provide a formal breakdown.
