South Korea's financial industry will mount its largest coordinated hiring event to date on August 19 and 20, when 82 institutions gather at the Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP) in Seoul for the 2026 Financial Sector Joint Recruitment Fair. The Financial Services Commission (FSC), which announced the plan on July 5, said the turnout is the biggest since the fair was first held in 2017.

## A Record Field of Participants

The scale of this year's event marks its defining feature. The 82 participating institutions span the full breadth of Korea's financial system, including banks, insurers, securities houses, card and capital firms, and state-run financial enterprises. According to the disclosed breakdown, the field includes 15 banks, 16 insurance companies and 9 securities firms, alongside card and capital lenders and public financial institutions.

Bringing these sectors together under one roof allows jobseekers to compare openings across the industry in a single visit—a contrast to the fragmented, firm-by-firm recruitment cycles that otherwise define financial hiring. The concentration of insurers and banks in particular signals where a large share of this year's vacancies is likely to sit.

## Why the Expanded Fair Matters

Since its 2017 debut, the joint fair has served as a barometer of hiring appetite across regulated finance. A record participant count suggests that, heading into the latter half of 2026, Korean financial institutions are collectively signaling active recruitment rather than retrenchment—an encouraging read for graduates and career-switchers targeting the sector.

The choice of the DDP, a high-traffic landmark in central Seoul, also reflects the FSC's intent to position the event as an accessible, industry-wide showcase rather than a niche recruiting drive. For candidates, the two-day format concentrates access to dozens of employers into a narrow window; for institutions, it offers a shared platform to compete for talent at a moment when the field of participants has never been wider.
